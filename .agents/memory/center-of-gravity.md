# Center of gravity = the SEAT, rendered as the Member OS

**Ruling:** the product's center of gravity is **the SEAT (member identity)**, rendered as
the **Member OS (`/my-syndicate`)**. Everything else orbits to give the seat meaning:
Economy funds/means it · Rank recognizes it · Chapter places it in history ·
Memory (artifacts/activity) remembers it · Proof verifies it · Future extends it.
The **homepage cockpit is the public TRAILER that promises the seat**, not the destination.

- One screen: Member OS · One symbol: Seat # + brand mark · One concept: "I have a seat."

**Why:** evaluated 9 candidates (Membership · Seat · Member OS · Vault · Activity · Rank ·
Chapter · NFT · Homepage) on uniqueness/emotion/legal-safety/already-built/memorability/
return-loop. Seat + Member OS win. **Vault is the legally riskiest anchor** (treasury ≠
member claim → ROI misread); **NFT carries NFT≠membership confusion**; Homepage is the
trailer, not where the product lives.

**Systems-not-pages model:** 5 systems — **Identity (core)**, Economy, Memory, Proof, Future;
pages are VIEWS of systems (re-group existing routes, **no new routes**). Every design
decision answers "which system is this a view of, and how does it serve the seat?"

**Sprint order ratified (this finding reinforces it):** 0 Brand/press kit (assets only, zero
protocol risk, do first) → 1 Homepage cockpit (un-hide ticker + lift engines) →
2 Member OS promotion (entry path + zero state + return-hook) → 3 Join simplification →
4 Nav/IA.

**How to apply:** when sequencing design/build, prioritize surfaces that make the seat
tangible/recognized/remembered; never let Vault, NFT, or token become the primary anchor;
the homepage's job is to promise the seat then funnel to Join. Captured on the architecture
board as Section I (Center of Gravity) + Section J (Five Systems); detail in
`docs/handoffs/CENTER_OF_GRAVITY_2026-06-17.md`.
