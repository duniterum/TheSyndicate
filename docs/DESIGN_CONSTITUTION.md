# THE SYNDICATE — DESIGN CONSTITUTION

**Status:** Director pass. No code. No components. No CSS. This document defines the universe before any page is redesigned.

**Doctrine that overrides everything below:** SYN is the seat. Artifacts are the memory. Chronicle is canon. Activity is the ledger. Do not trust — verify. Watch a protocol operate in public.

---

## 1. The Emotional Product

The Syndicate is not a token, not a dashboard, not a community. The product is **the feeling of holding a permanent position inside a protocol that is visibly becoming history in real time.**

Three emotional pillars, in priority order:

1. **Permanence** — "What I joined cannot be undone, re-issued, or diluted. My seat is mine, sealed at a block height that will never repeat."
2. **Witness** — "I can see the protocol operate. Every claim is one click from proof. Nothing is told to me — everything is shown."
3. **Anticipation** — "Something specific is about to be sealed. I know what, I know when, I know my place in it."

If a surface does not produce one of these three feelings in under five seconds, it is decoration and must be removed or demoted.

---

## 2. The Visual Identity

**The Syndicate looks like an institution being built in public.** Not a startup. Not a memecoin. Not a club. The closest cultural reference is a **central bank that publishes its ledger live**, or a **museum whose acquisitions log is the front door**.

Four visual constants:

- **Editorial weight** — Instrument Serif headlines carry meaning. Headlines are sentences, not slogans. The serif tells the reader: this is a record, not a pitch.
- **Monospaced truth** — every number that comes from a chain is set in JetBrains Mono. The eye learns: mono = verifiable, sans = human, serif = canonical.
- **Two semantic colors only** — gold (membership, seat, seal, premium CTA) and verify cyan (live, on-chain, proof). Success/warning/error exist but are functional, never decorative. No gradients used as identity. No accent purples, no neon, no glass.
- **Ivory by default, cinematic black on demand** — light mode is the institutional posture (museum, ledger, registry). Dark mode is the cinematic posture (archive vault, Chronicle, seal moments). Both are first-class; neither is "the night mode of the other".

Negative space is a feature. Density is earned by data, never by ornament.

## 3. The Three Audiences and What They Feel

### First-time visitor (0–60 seconds)
- Lands on the homepage and within one screen reads, in this order: *what this is*, *what is happening right now*, *what is verifiable*, *what I would become if I joined*.
- Feels: **"This is not pretending. The numbers are live. The seats are finite. I am late but not too late."**
- Leaves with one of two intents: become a member, or come back to watch.

### Connected member (recurring visit)
- Lands on My Syndicate. Within one screen reads: *my seat number, my chapter, what changed since I left, what is about to be sealed next.*
- Feels: **"My position is intact. The protocol moved while I was gone. I am still early."**
- Returns because there is always a "what changed since last visit" answer anchored to a block height.

### Long-term member (5 years in)
- Lands on My Syndicate and sees a **deepening record**: chapters they witnessed, events they co-witnessed, artifacts whose eligibility was sealed by their seat number, names of cohort bands they belong to.
- Feels: **"I am part of the canon. My early seat is now historical. The story I joined is now a chapter in a longer one."**
- Treats the page like a passport, not a dashboard.

## 4. Permanent Domain Model

The Syndicate has **six permanent domains.** Every route, every page, every module the protocol will ever ship must belong to exactly one. New ideas that do not fit a domain are rejected or postponed.

| # | Domain | One-line definition | Permanent surfaces |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Protocol** | The live, verifiable operation of the system. | Homepage, Activity, Registry, Transparency, Protocol Health, B2B |
| 2 | **Identity** | The seat as a first-class noun. | My Syndicate, SeatRecord721, Ranks, Reputation, Member wall |
| 3 | **Memory** | Sealed history that cannot be rewritten. | Chronicle, Archive1155, Chapters, Episodes, Founders Hall |
| 4 | **Assets** | What the protocol controls and how it routes value. | Tokenomics, Treasury, Liquidity, Vault, Use of Funds |
| 5 | **Growth** | How members extend the protocol. | Membership Sale, Referral, Marketplace, Quests |
| 6 | **Future** | What is coming, anchored to real thresholds. | Roadmap, Governance, AI, deferred modules |

Domains are not navigation labels — they are an editorial commitment. The nav exposes the surfaces; the domain decides the tone.

## 5. Visual Hierarchy of the Ecosystem

Three tiers. Everything sorts into one.

### Hero tier (the protocol's face)
- **The Seat** (My Syndicate identity block)
- **The Live Ledger** (Activity heartbeat — what is happening *right now*)
- **The Chronicle entry currently being written** (the open chapter)
- **The Total Protocol Revenue Generated** number

These are the only four things allowed to dominate a screen. Everything else orbits.

### Secondary tier (proof and depth)
- Treasury composition, routing flow, registry, holder index, rank ladder, artifact catalog, referral state, sale state, governance preview.
- These are *invited in* when the hero has been answered. They render with restraint: tables, mono numerals, status pills, source links.

### Tertiary tier (never dominates)
- FAQ, risk, docs, whitepaper, terminology, legal, deferred modules, AI infrastructure, lab experiments, marketing copy.
- Always reachable, never on the hero. Tertiary content that tries to dominate a hero is the single most common failure mode of this site.

## 7. Visual Language That Connects Every Page

Five connective threads. Any page that breaks one breaks the constitution.

1. **The status pill is universal.** Every data cell on every page carries LIVE · PARTIAL · PENDING. A page without status pills is not a Syndicate page.
2. **Every number is one click from its source.** No exceptions. Mono numerals are *always* links to an explorer, a contract, or a sealed event.
3. **Headlines are sentences in Instrument Serif.** UI labels are Work Sans. Numbers and addresses are JetBrains Mono. No fourth typeface exists.
4. **Gold means seat. Cyan means verify.** Never blended, never repurposed.
5. **Every page answers "what changed since last visit"** — anchored to a block height, not a timestamp.

## 9. What Is Helping / What Is Hurting

**Helping (keep):**
- Light-default + dark-toggle theme system; gold/cyan semantic split.
- Instrument Serif + Work Sans + JetBrains Mono trio.
- Status pills (LIVE/PARTIAL/PENDING), source links on every number.
- The five constitutional gates (Decision Lenses, Multi-Hat, Infinite Narrative, Core Asset, Mythology).
- The seat-as-noun framing and member-number primacy.

**Hurting (stop):**
- Pages built as **stacks of components** instead of compositions. The site repeatedly fails because sections are added, never arranged.
- **Module pluralism on the hero** — too many tier-2 elements competing for tier-1 attention on Homepage and My Syndicate.
- **Dashboard reflex** — when in doubt, the team adds another card grid. The protocol is not a dashboard; it is a record.
- **Educational copy where proof should be** — explanations of how things work crowd out the things actually working.
- **Accordion/tab inflation** — hiding hierarchy inside disclosure widgets instead of making decisions.
- **Iteration on existing layouts** rather than starting from the editorial question "what is this page *for*?"

## 10. Final Mental Model

> **The Syndicate is a protocol you join the way you join a society: by taking a seat that is sealed on-chain, and then watching — and being part of — the record it writes.**

In one image: a **museum whose ledger is open to the public, whose acquisitions log updates live, and whose members are listed by the block height at which they took their seat.**

What a world-class design jury would call unique:

- **Truth as aesthetic.** Most crypto sites perform credibility; this one *displays* it. Mono numerals as hyperlinks to chain proof is a visual signature.
- **Permanence over performance.** The seat is the product. Tokens, payouts, governance, even artifacts orbit a *position*, not a price.
- **An editorial protocol.** Serif headlines, sealed chapters, named cohorts, and a Chronicle that is canon — the Syndicate reads like a publication that happens to be a contract.
- **Restraint at scale.** Two semantic colors, three typefaces, six domains, three hero objects. Most protocols add; this one composes.

## 11. My Syndicate, Inside This Universe

(Short on purpose. The page is not redesigned here — its role is defined.)

Within the constitution above, **My Syndicate is the member's passport into the Identity domain.** It is the *only* page where the seat is allowed to occupy the full hero. Everything else on the page exists to answer four questions, in this order:

1. **Who am I in this protocol?** — member number, chapter of joining, cohort axis, founder flag, block-height anchor, co-witness set. Hero tier.
2. **What changed since I last visited?** — a single timeline anchored to block heights, merging routing, activity, and "what changed". Secondary tier.
3. **What is about to be sealed that involves me?** — the Horizon: SOON / NEXT / FAR, each anchored to a real on-chain threshold (e.g. "Chapter I closes at №333"). Secondary tier.
4. **What memory do I already hold, and what is within reach?** — artifacts as visual objects with three states (held / eligible / locked). Secondary tier.

What My Syndicate is **not**:
- Not a dashboard of every protocol metric (those live on Homepage and Activity).
- Not a referral console (Referral lives in the Growth domain and links in).
- Not an education page (FAQ/docs live in tertiary).
- Not a governance surface (Governance is its own room).
- Not a marketplace (Marketplace is its own room).

The page's job is to make a returning member feel **"my seat is intact, the protocol moved, and I know exactly what is next."** Nothing more. Everything else is a link out.
