**Scope:** LIVE SITE ONLY (https://thesyndicate.money) — what a visitor sees today.
**Method:** Each surface evaluated through the 13 constitutional hats, then scored on
the Five-Value Test (0–10 each, 50 max). No source code, no planned changes, no docs.

- **EXCELLENT** ≥ 40
- **GOOD** 30–39
- **WEAK** 20–29

## 2. Headline diagnoses

- **Engineering is the strongest axis on every single surface.** No surface scores
  below 6 on Engineering. This confirms the framework's central thesis: the
  remaining debt is not technical.
- **Emotional + Retention scores collapse below 5 on 17 of 24 surfaces.**
- **Story scores are bimodal:** strong where narrative components exist
  (Story So Far, Founders, Chapter detail), near-zero where they don't
  (Activity, Liquidity, Roadmap, Token, Tokenomics).
- **No surface scores EXCELLENT.** Ceiling today is GOOD (35, Story So Far).

### 3.2 Top 10 highest-leverage improvements
(Ranked by emotional + retention upside per unit of implementation cost.)

1. **Hero** — reframe from "explain transparency" to "sell desire". Highest traffic surface, lowest emotional score per visitor.
2. **Coming Next** — convert from informational note to anticipation engine (named, dated-or-event-triggered, consequential).
3. **/roadmap** — rewrite cards from builder-backlog ("Docs Hub") to story milestones ("What changes for a member when this ships").
4. **/activity** — promote *significance* (new member, chapter close, milestone, vault flow) above chronology.
5. **Identity Zone (disconnected)** — replace dashboard scaffolding with a "could-be" preview that makes early-access tangible.
6. **/liquidity** — wrap the action rail in *meaning*: why LP exists in the story, why deeper LP matters for every member.
7. **/chapters index** — visualize the infinite arc (Chapter 5, 20, 100 sealed/PENDING) so the system never reads "finite".
8. **Why Join** — reduce to one block whose verbs are aspirational and whose proofs are one click away.
9. **/registry** — frame as "the public record of who showed up", not as a CSV of addresses.
10. **/members** — surface participation/longevity signals, not address lists.

## 5. Calibration notes (for future scorecards)

- Emotional / Story / Retention scores remain partly subjective. Re-score
  every major surface after each ship; track movement, not absolute values.
- Engineering ≥ 6 on every surface is the floor the constitution already
  guarantees — it is *not* a sign of progress. Future scorecards should
  weight Emotional + Retention more heavily when ranking leverage.
- The framework should be re-run after each top-5 ship to verify the
  predicted lift materializes; if a redesign lands without moving the
  scorecard, the redesign itself failed the framework.
